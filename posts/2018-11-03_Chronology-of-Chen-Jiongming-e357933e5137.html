Chronology of Chen Jiongming

January 18, 1878 Chen Jiongming born in Haifeng, Guangdong
August 1908 Chen graduates from Academy of Law and Political Science at Canton
October 14, 1909 First annual session, Guangdong Provincial Assembly
February 12, 1910 Revolt of the Canton New Army
February 1910–October 1911 Assassination Corps active
May 23, 1910 First extraordinary session, Guangdong Provincial Assembly
October 3, 1910 Second annual session, Guangdong Provincial Assembly
March-April 1911 Publication of the revolutionary journal Ke bao
March 22-April 13, 1911 Second extraordinary session, Guangdong Provincial Assembly
March 30, 1911 Canton celebrates gambling-suppression victory
April 27, 1911 Canton uprising
October 10, 1911 The Wuchang Revolt begins
October 22, 1911 Third annual session, Guangdong Provincial Assembly
November 6, 1911 The battle at Huizhou is engaged
November 9, 1911 Guangdong declares independence
November 1911-August 1913 First Republican administration in Canton established
July 18-August 4,1913 The Second Revolution begins — First campaign against Yuan Shikai
August 1913-December 1915 Chen exiled in Malaysia
January 18, 1915 Japan pressures Yuan’s government with Twenty-one Demands
February 25, 1915 Chen joins with Huang Xing et al. to halt anti-Yuan campaign
October 15. 1915 Chen explains why he declines to join Sun’s new party in Japan
December 15. 1915 Yuan announces his intention to become emperor
January-June 1916 Second anti-Yuan campaign
June 6, 1916 Yuan Shikai dies — end of the Second Revolution
June 23, 1917 Constitution Protection movement begins
June-August, 1918 The Fujian campaign
August 1918-November 1920 Chen administers “Little Model China” in southern Fujian
April 20, 1919 First emissary arrives from Soviet Russia
May 4, 1919 New Culture movement begins
August-November, 1920 Chen initiates the campaign to return to Guangdong
November 29, 1920 Chen welcomes Sun Yat-sen back to Canton
November 1920-January 1923 Chen administers the reconstruction of Guangdong
May 5, 1921 Sun is inaugurated as president-extraordinary in Canton
June-September, 1921 The Guangxi campaign
August 18, 1921 The qiao telegram episode
October 15, 1921 Sun leaves Canton on the Northern Expedition
March 21, 1922 Chen’s chief of staff, General Deng Keng, assassinated
April 1922 Sun suddenly returns to Canton and forces Chen to retire to East Guangdong
May 16, 1922 Hong Kong newspapers publish Chen’s reconstruction plan
June 1–2, 1922 Old parliament is restored and Northern president Xu Shichang resigns
June 3, 1922 President of Peking University appeals to Sun to resign from the Southern presidency
June 14, 1922 Guangdong Army requests Sun’s resignation from the Southern presidency
June 16–17, 1922 June 16 Incident: Guangdong Army rebels in Canton and Sun bombards the city
August 8, 1922 Sun leaves Canton
August 15, 1922 Chen returns to Canton
January 1923 Sun’s mercenary troops invade Guangdong and Chen is forced to retire a second time to East Guangdong
May 28-October 27, 1923 The siege of Huizhou
July-August 1923 Chen campaigns into southern Fujian
November 18–19, 1923 Battle of the Canton suburbs
April-September 1924 Wu Zhihui tries to reconcile Chen and Sun
May 1924 First Swatow Conference
October 15, 1924 The Xiguan Massacre
October-December 1924 Cantonese movement to expel Sun
November 1924 Second Swatow Conference — Chen plans to retake Canton
March-April 1925 First East River campaign
February 28, 1925 Chen’s representatives attend meetings of Zhigong Association in Victoria, Canada
March 12, 1925 Sun Yat-sen dies
June-July 1925 Tang Jiyao’s Yunnanese Federalist Army defeated
June-November 1925 Second East River campaign
October 10, 1925 Zhigong Association formally reorganized as Zhigong Party of the Americas-Chen elected as chairman and Tang Jiyao of Yunnan as vice-chairman
February 1926 Headquarters of Zhigong Party moves to Hong Kong — Intense effort to extend recruitment among overseas Chinese in Southeast Asia.
July 1926 Chiang Kai-shek’s Northern Expeditionary Army enters Hunan — The Federalist movement in mainland China is defeated
Winter 1927 Chen publishes A Proposal for the Unification of China
December 1928 Chen meets with Duan Qirui and Northern leaders to form a Grand Republican Alliance
September 18, 1931 The Mukden Incident — Japanese forces invade Manchuria
December 1, 1931 Chen publishes an open letter to the Chinese people
September 22, 1933 Chen dies in Hong Kong
April 3, 1935 Chen is buried in Huizhou, Guangdong

(Excerpt from Chen Jiongming and the federalist movement by Leslie H. Dingyan Chen)
