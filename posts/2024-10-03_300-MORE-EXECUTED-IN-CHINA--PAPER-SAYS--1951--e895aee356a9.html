300 MORE EXECUTED IN CHINA, PAPER SAYS (1951)

HONG KONG, Feb. 14-The independent newspaper, Wah Kiu Yat Po, which claims to have reporters operating under cover on the China mainland, said today that the Communists had shot 300 persons in a mass execution at Swatow last Friday.

This dispatch could not be confirmed, but if true it would be the largest mainland execution yet reported here. The largest number of persons reported executed at one time by the Communists themselves in recent months is fortytwo.. The Reds also have announced the execution of “Nationalist agents” in groups of six, nine and fifteen.

During the last week in January, the Chinese press here, including Communist-supervised newspapers, published reports of a house-tohouse round-up of “suspected elements” in Swatow. At that time, the Communists held that many agents were entering China from Formosa through the North Kwangtung port, and said that 600 persons had been taken into custody.

Wah Kiu Yat Po also reported that the Reds had seized a Nationalist “guerrilla control center” on Tao Island, off Swatow, on Feb. 5.

New York Time, February 15, 1951
