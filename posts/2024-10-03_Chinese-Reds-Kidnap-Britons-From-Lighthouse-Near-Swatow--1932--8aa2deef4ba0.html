Chinese Reds Kidnap Britons From Lighthouse Near Swatow (1932)

Special Cable to THE NEW YORK TIMES.

HONGKONG, Tuesday, March 1. -Communist raiders on Saturday carried off the staff of foreign keepers of the Breaker Point Lighthouse, thirty miles southwest of Swatow, according to news received by the British naval authorities at Hongkong.

The captives include John Edwards and his wife and four children, British subjects, and no news of their fate has been received since they were kidnapped. Chinese keepers are maintaining the light.

The British warship Whitehall, stationed at Swatow, is cooperating with Chinese authorities in efforts to rescue the victims and apprehend the Communists, while the warship Keppel is rushing from Hongkong to Swatow.

New York Times, March 1, 1932
