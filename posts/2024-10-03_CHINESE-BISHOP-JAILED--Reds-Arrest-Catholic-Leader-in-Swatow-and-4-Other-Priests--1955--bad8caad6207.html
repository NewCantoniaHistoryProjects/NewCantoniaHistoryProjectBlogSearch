CHINESE BISHOP JAILED; Reds Arrest Catholic Leader in Swatow and 4 Other Priests (1955)

HONG KONG, Dec. 23 (AP) The Roman Catholic Bishop of Swatow and four other Chinese Catholic priests have been arrested in a new anti-religion campaign by the Chinese Communist Government, reports from Peiping today said.

The reports said the Communists had made a sweep through China’s large cities, arresting missionaries and Christian laymen who still attended church services despite previous Red campaigns against religion. The reports charged that Bishop Huang Ku-shun had “cursed” the Communist Government as “the devil’s Government.”

All five priests were jailed as “counter-revolutionists,” the Chinese Communists said.

New York Time, December 24, 1955
